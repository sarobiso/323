6.3 The Method of Distribution Functions

Example 6.1

A process for refining sugar yields up to 1 ton of pure sugar per day, but the actual amount produced, \(X\) , is a random variable because of machine breakdowns and other slowdowns. Suppose that \(X\) has density function given by

\[\begin{align}f_X(x)=\begin{cases} 2x,& 0\le x\le 1\\ 0,& \text{otherwise }\\ \end{cases} \end{align}\]

The company is paid at the rate of $300 per ton for the refined sugar, but it also has a fixed overhead cost of $100 per day. Thus the daily profit, in hundreds of dollars, is \(Y = 3X − 1\). Find the probability density function for \(Y\).

Additional Examples

Let \(X\) be a continuous random variable with the following probability density function: \(f(x)=3x^2\) for \(0<x<1\). What is the probability density function of \(Y=X^2\),\(f(y)\)?

Let \(X\) be a continuous random variable that follows an Exponential distribution, with mean \(=\frac{1}{2}\). What is the probability density function of \(Y=2X+1\),\(f(y)\)?

Let \(X_1\) and \(X_2\) be two independent exponentially distributed random variables both with mean \(\beta=1\). What is the probability density function of \(Y=X_1+X_2\),\(f(y)\)?

A holding tank has a capacity of 5000 litres is to be stocked at the beginning of the week. Because of various factors, when the tank is replenished it is not necessarily filled to capacity. As a result, the amount in this tank at the beginning of the week is a random variable \(X\) that is measured in 1000s of litres.

The amount removed from the tank over the course of the week - the demand - fluctuates from week to week as well. Therefore, the amount removed in a week is a random variable \(Y\).

The joint probability density function of \(X\) and \(Y\) is given by:

\[\begin{align}f_{X,Y}(x,y)=\begin{cases} \frac{3x}{125},& 0\le y\le x \le 5\\ 0,& \text{otherwise }\\ \end{cases} \end{align}\]

Prior to the tank being refilled, a measurement is taken to record how much remains in the tank. The amount remaining is a random variable, expressed as a function of \(X\) and $Y.

Let Z represent the amount of gasoline remaining in the underground tank, or \(Z = X- Y\). Find the probability density function of \(Z\),\(f_Z(z)\).
